Kelly Formula

f* = (b x p - q) / b
b = decimal odds - 1
p = market prob + estimated edge
q = 1 - p

Bet = Bankroll x f* x Fraction
How edge is estimated
Base edge depends on your mode setting. Each additional signal on the same game adds +1% edge (up to +3%). More signals = higher confidence = larger position.

The idea is simple: Sometimes a team is winning because of things that won't last â€” like making every 3-pointer, or getting way more free throws than normal. When that happens, the live betting odds are wrong because they think the score is "real." We catch that.

How to use it:

Step 1: Hit Start to auto-refresh. The dashboard watches every live game.

How This Dashboard Works

Every signal in this dashboard shares one thesis: the current score was built on something unsustainable, so the live moneyline is mispriced. Live odds react to the scoreboard, but they fail to distinguish between sustainable scoring (paint, mid-range) and unsustainable scoring (hot 3PT streaks, star underperformance). The 2-signal system (3PT Fragile + Star Coil) plus Combined Edge identifies when these unsustainable conditions exist â€” that is the edge.

1. 3PT Fragile Lead (Merged Signal)

What it detects: This is the most selective signal â€” it requires BOTH of two conditions to be true simultaneously:

Condition 1 â€” Hot 3PT Shooting: A team shooting 50%+ from three-point range on 12+ attempts (or 55%+ on 8+). The NBA league average is ~36.5%. This rate is 2+ standard deviations above the mean and will regress.

Condition 2 â€” Fragile Lead Composition: The same team's lead is 42%+ dependent on 3PT scoring AND the trailing team scores from the paint (58%+ non-3PT). The lead is not just hot â€” it's structurally dependent on that hot shooting.

Why both conditions matter: Hot 3PT shooting alone doesn't mean the lead is fragile â€” the team might also be scoring from the paint. And a fragile lead composition alone doesn't mean the shooting will regress â€” the percentage might be within normal range. Only when BOTH are true is the edge genuine: the shooting WILL cool off AND the lead DEPENDS on it.

Trigger: 3PT% ≥50% on ≥12 attempts (or 55%+ on 8+) AND lead composition ≥42% from 3PT AND opponent ≥58% non-3PT AND ≥20 total points AND lead between 3-15 pts. All conditions must be met for Level 2 (strong) signal. The 3-15pt margin filter ensures the lead is real but erasable â€” not a blowout, not a dead heat.

When only one condition is met: If only the 3PT shooting is hot (no fragile lead) or only the lead is fragile (shooting not extreme), the system shows a Level 1 warning but does NOT fire the full signal.

âš ï¸ Opponent Engine Check (NEW): Even when 3PT Fragile fires, the system checks: can the trailing team actually score without relying on the opponent's regression? It computes the trailing team's non-3PT points per minute (paint + FT + mid-range). If below 1.3 PPM, their offense is fundamentally broken â€” even if the leading team's 3s cool down, the trailing team has no engine to capitalize. The signal is downgraded to âš ï¸ (weaker). Regression only matters if the other team can take advantage of it.

2. Star Coil (Upgraded Star Cold)

What it detects: A franchise-caliber player performing well below their expected scoring pace (<65%) during Q2-Q3 AND the game is competitive (within 15 points). This is the "compressed spring" â€” the star is coiled and will break out, and because the game is close, that breakout changes the outcome.

Why it is an edge: Star players regress upward. Star Coil requires: (1) game must be competitive so the star's breakout actually impacts the result, and (2) timing must be Q2-Q3 â€” the sweet spot where regression has time to play out.

ðŸ”’ Damage Locked (NEW): But what if the star heats up and the game result still doesn't change? The system tracks the score over consecutive refresh cycles (~30s each). If the star's team has been trailing AND the deficit stayed flat or grew over 3+ checks, it flags "ðŸ”’ Damage Locked." The cold stretch already did its damage â€” even if the star catches fire now, the hole is too deep. The bet direction FLIPS: instead of betting on the star's team (expecting breakout to flip the game), you bet against them (the deficit is baked in).

Trigger: Star's actual production <65% of expected pace AND Q2 or Q3 AND score margin ≤15 pts.

ðŸ”’ Damage Locked: Star's team trailing + deficit stable or growing over 3+ consecutive score snapshots. Bet direction flips â€” fade the star's team.

3. Combined Edge

What it detects: When both 3PT Fragile and Star Coil fire on the same game. These two signals measure genuinely different dimensions of unsustainability â€” no overlap or double-counting.

How it works: The system checks for Level 2 signals on both types. When both fire, the thesis elevates to signalLevel = 2 (strong combined).

3PT Fragile + Star Coil: Opponent's lead is built on hot 3PT shooting AND their star is cold in a close game. When the star heats up and 3PT% drops, momentum shifts hard. This is the maximum edge scenario.

Bet Side Logic (ML Signals)

The system scores each side's "unsustainability count" with two key modifiers:

  • 3PT Fragile + Opponent Engine: If the trailing team can't score (non-3PT PPM < 1.3), the fade signal is weakened â€” regression won't help a broken offense.
  • Star Coil + Damage Locked: If the star's team deficit is stable/growing over 3+ checks, the bet flips â€” fade the star's team instead of betting on them.

The side with more fade reasons gets faded; the system recommends the opposite side â€” the team whose advantages are real, sustainable, and undervalued.

Data Sources & Limitations

All data comes from ESPN's live scoreboard API (scores, period/clock, team stats, leader boards) and ESPN's summary endpoint (FTA, box score details). Refreshes on configurable interval (default 30s).

Not captured: Individual player foul counts, play-by-play data, lineup changes, injury updates, referee tendencies, pace-of-play, and real-time odds. The system focuses on what IS measurable and predictive: shooting rates, scoring composition, free throw attempts, and star production.

Important: No signal guarantees an outcome. These are probabilistic edges based on mean reversion and market inefficiency. Responsible bankroll management is essential.
