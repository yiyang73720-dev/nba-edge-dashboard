Signal Guide

All signals share one thesis: the current score was built on something unsustainable, so the live moneyline is mispriced.

3PT 3PT Regression

The Edge: Unsustainable Shooting Regresses to Mean

When a team shoots far above their season 3PT%, regression is statistically inevitable. The lead built on hot 3PT shooting is inflated — teams shooting 50%+ from 3 in a half almost never sustain it.

Trigger: 50%+ on 12+ attempts OR 55%+ on 8+ attempts = strong signal. Elevated: 45%+ on 10+ attempts.

STAR Star Cold

The Edge: Cold Stars Regress Upward

When a star player (23+ PPG) is significantly below their expected scoring pace, regression upward is likely. The market undervalues their team while the star is cold, creating a buy-low window.

Trigger: Star at <60% of expected scoring pace, at least 20% of game elapsed, Q1-Q3.

FRAGILE Fragile Lead

The Edge: 3PT-Heavy Lead vs Paint-Heavy Trail = Fade the Lead

Fires only when BOTH conditions are true: the leading team's scoring is 3PT-heavy (42%+ from 3) AND the trailing team's scoring is paint-heavy (58%+ non-3PT). The lead is inflated and the trailing team's offense is sustainable.

Trigger: Leading team 42%+ pts from 3PT AND trailing team 58%+ pts from non-3PT (min 20 pts scored).

FT FT Inflation

The Edge: Leads Built on Free Throw Volume Regress

When a leading team has significantly more free throw attempts than the trailing team, part of their lead is built on unsustainable FT volume. FT rates regress within a game — refs tend to even out calls.

Trigger: Leading team has 10+ more FTA than trailing team. FTA data from ESPN summary endpoint.

COMBINED Combined Edge

The Edge: Multiple Signals Compound the Mispricing

When 2+ of the above signals fire on the same game, the live moneyline is mispriced on multiple dimensions simultaneously. Each signal is an independent confirmation that the current score is built on unsustainable conditions.

Trigger: 2+ of {3PT, Star Cold, Fragile Lead, FT Inflation} active on same game = strong combined edge.

All signals share one thesis: the current score was built on something unsustainable, so the live moneyline is mispriced. NCAA-calibrated thresholds for college basketball.

3PT 3PT Regression

The Edge: College Shooting Regresses Faster

NCAA teams have higher variance in 3PT shooting due to younger players and less consistent mechanics. Hot shooting spells regress even more sharply than in the NBA. League avg ~33.4%.

Trigger: 50%+ on 12+ attempts OR 55%+ on 8+ attempts. College shooters sustain hot streaks even less than pros.

TEAM Team Offensive Slump

The Edge: College Teams Lack Depth to Compensate

In NCAA, when the primary scorer goes cold, the bench drop-off is severe. College teams lack NBA-level depth, making individual cold stretches more impactful on game outcomes.

Trigger: Key scorer at <60% expected pace, at least 20% of game elapsed.

FRAGILE Fragile Lead

The Edge: 3PT-Dependent Leads Collapse Faster in College

Fires when leading team's scoring is 3PT-heavy (38%+ from 3 in NCAA) AND trailing team scores from the paint (58%+ non-3PT). College shooters are less consistent, making these leads even more fragile.

Trigger: Leading team 38%+ pts from 3PT AND trailing team 58%+ pts from non-3PT (min 20 pts).

FT FT Inflation

The Edge: FT Disparity Inflates Scores in College

NCAA's earlier bonus situation (7th foul) and 5-foul limit create more FT opportunities. When a leading team has a large FTA advantage, that lead is partially built on unsustainable free throw volume.

Trigger: Leading team has 10+ more FTA than trailing team. Refs tend to even out calls over the game.

COMBINED Combined Edge

The Edge: Signal Stacking in Higher-Variance Games

NCAA games have more variance, making combined signals even more powerful. When 2+ signals fire on the same game, the mispricing compounds because college games are more prone to dramatic swings.

Trigger: 2+ of {3PT, Star Cold, Fragile Lead, FT Inflation} active on same game.

How This Dashboard Works

Every signal in this dashboard shares one thesis: the current score was built on something unsustainable, so the live moneyline is mispriced. Live odds react to the scoreboard, but they fail to distinguish between sustainable scoring (paint, mid-range) and unsustainable scoring (hot 3PT streaks, FT inflation). The 5-signal system (3PT Regression, Star Cold, Fragile Lead, FT Inflation, Combined Edge) identifies when these unsustainable conditions exist — that is the edge.

1. 3PT Regression Signal

What it detects: A team shooting 50%+ from three-point range on 8 or more attempts. The NBA league average is ~36.5% and NCAA is ~33.4%. When a team is 15+ percentage points above average on significant volume, mean reversion is statistically inevitable.

Why it is an edge: Three-point shooting is the most volatile stat in basketball. Even elite shooters regress heavily within a single game. A team hitting 9-of-16 from three in the first half will almost certainly cool off. If their lead is built on this hot streak, the market is pricing in a scoring pace that will not continue. The team that earned points inside the arc (paint scoring, free throws from driving) has the more durable lead.

Trigger: 3PT% ≥50% AND 3PT made ≥8 → Level 2 (strong). The further above league average, the stronger the regression expectation. The signal creates thesis criteria tracking which side is shooting unsustainably hot.

What to watch for: The signal is strongest when the hot-shooting team is leading by a margin smaller than their 3PT-inflated points. If a team made 12 threes at 55% (vs their normal 35%), roughly 6 of those threes are "above expected" — that is ~18 points of inflated scoring.

2. Star Player Cold / Below Pace

What it detects: A franchise-caliber player performing well below their expected scoring pace early in the game (Q1-Q3 for NBA). The system tracks each team's leading scorer and compares their actual output to their per-minute expected production.

Why it is an edge: Star players regress upward. A player averaging 28 PPG who has 6 points through two quarters is not suddenly a bad player — they are having an off stretch that will likely correct. If their team is losing partly because their star is cold, the market is undervaluing that team's true offensive potential. The star will get more touches, the coach will run plays for them, and production typically surges in the second half.

Trigger: Star's actual production <60% of expected pace AND usage ≥20% of team scoring AND Q3 or earlier. The system calculates expected output using per-minute projections from season averages. Example: A 30 PPG scorer expected to have 20 pts by halftime who only has 8 — that gap will likely close.

Nuance: The "star cold" teams list (starColdTeams) is tracked to factor into the combined thesis. If a star is cold AND their team is trailing, the combined system interprets this as a buy-low opportunity.

3. Fragile Lead

What it detects: A dual condition: the leading team's scoring is 3PT-heavy (42%+ of points from 3) AND the trailing team's scoring is paint-heavy (58%+ from non-3PT). This is the "fade the lead" signal.

Why it is an edge: The lead is built on volatile 3PT shooting that will regress, while the trailing team earns points from sustainable paint scoring. The market sees the scoreboard but not the scoring composition.

Trigger: Leading team has ≥42% of points from 3PT (NBA; 38% NCAA) AND trailing team has ≥58% from non-3PT AND ≥20 total points. Both conditions must be true — this filters noise from single-condition checks.

Key insight: This pairs powerfully with 3PT regression. If the leading team's 3PT% is also unsustainably high (50%+), you have two independent confirmations that the lead will erode.

4. FT Inflation

What it detects: When the leading team has significantly more free throw attempts than the trailing team, indicating their lead is partly built on unsustainable FT volume.

Why it is an edge: A large FTA gap means the leading team is getting to the line at an abnormal rate. Free throw rates regress within a game — referees tend to even out calls. The FT-inflated portion of the lead is not built on genuine offensive superiority.

Trigger: Leading team has 10+ more FTA than the trailing team. FTA data is fetched from ESPN's summary endpoint (same source used for box score stats).

5. Combined Edge

What it detects: When 2+ of the 4 signals (3PT, Star Cold, Fragile Lead, FT Inflation) fire on the same game. Multiple independent signals confirm the live moneyline is mispriced on multiple dimensions.

How stacking works: The system checks for Level 2 signals across all 4 types. Any 2+ active signals elevates the thesis to signalLevel = 2 (strong combined).

Strongest combos:

3PT + Fragile Lead: Team shooting hot AND lead built on those threes. Double confirmation the lead will evaporate.

Star Cold + FT Inflation: Star underperforming AND opponent lead inflated by FTs. Both forces favor the trailing team.

Fragile Lead + FT Inflation: Lead is both 3PT-dependent AND FT-inflated. Two separate unsustainable foundations.

3PT + Star Cold: Opponent shooting unsustainably hot AND their star is cold. When the star heats up and 3PT% drops, momentum shifts hard.

Bet Side Logic

The system scores each side's "unsustainability count." Away shooting hot = awayFade++. Away lead fragile = awayFade++. Away lead FT-inflated = awayFade++. Star cold on a team = bet ON that team (star regresses upward = fade the other side). The side with more fade reasons gets faded; the system recommends the opposite side — the team whose advantages are real, sustainable, and undervalued.

Data Sources & Limitations

All data comes from ESPN's live scoreboard API (scores, period/clock, team stats, leader boards) and ESPN's summary endpoint (FTA, box score details). Refreshes on configurable interval (default 30s).

Not captured: Individual player foul counts, play-by-play data, lineup changes, injury updates, referee tendencies, pace-of-play, and real-time odds. The system focuses on what IS measurable and predictive: shooting rates, scoring composition, free throw attempts, and star production.

Important: No signal guarantees an outcome. These are probabilistic edges based on mean reversion and market inefficiency. Responsible bankroll management is essential.
